BMTBattleMech Tournament — Complete Rulebook v1.0
Game Overview
Large-scale BattleTech planetary combat — fast, combined arms, faction-driven.
Design Goals: BMT simulates large-scale BattleTech battles at planetary scale. Battles are fast-paced, feel authentically BattleTech, take place across hexagon world maps, and include full combined arms: infantry, battle armor, tanks, artillery, 'Mechs, and air support.
Core Concepts
The Lance is the Atom
Individual units matter less than lances. A lance moves, fights, and takes damage as a single unit. A lance is either Functional, Crippled (below 50% strength), or Destroyed. All IP events are measured at lance level.
Dual Resource System
PV (Budget) — what you can afford to field, measured in Alpha Strike Point Value.
IP (Influence Points) — Political Clout, gates what formations you can requisition and unlocks special actions.
Minimum Battle Scale
The minimum engagement is Company vs Company (12 'Mechs per side). Each hexagon on the world map represents a single BattleTech map sheet. Lances are placed on hexes and move as single units.
Alpha Strike Cards
Every lance or star is built using Alpha Strike Cards. Each card flags the lance's weapon classification (E / M / A), PV total, movement, and damage ratings used to calculate Damage Dice.
Damage Dice System
Every 2 Damage = 1 Damage Die
Each Damage Die = one pair of D6 for attack purposes.

Example: Lance A has an overall attack rating of 6, giving it 2 Damage Dice.
Lance A rolls 2 pairs of D6. Both attacks succeed — result: 4 damage from one pair, 2 from the other.
If one attack fails, the maximum damage is allotted to the successful attack (in this case, 4 damage).

Lance Actions (Choose One Per Lance Per Turn)
Attack
Full offensive. Damage dice applied vs target lance in same or adjacent hex.
Move
Reposition on world map. No attacks this turn. Can capture uncontested hex objectives.
Retreat
Move at half speed. Half the lance can shoot and take damage. Must be declared in Phase 1 or costs −2 IP.
Scan Objective
Gather intel on a hex. Reveals hidden GM bonus objectives worth +3 IP on capture.
Target Tag — Recon Lances only
Designates enemy lance for bonus damage next turn. Artillery and air support can strike tagged targets without line of sight. Tagging lance earns +1 bonus IP if the tagged lance is destroyed.

Phase 6 — Requisition Window
After IP is tallied, the Requisition Window opens. Spend IP to pay the formation cost — unit arrives next turn. Spend PV to equip with specific units. Bank IP for a higher tier or larger requisition. Veto (3 IP) to block one opponent requisition this window.
IP — Influence Points (Political Clout)
IP gates what you can requisition. It is both a tier threshold and a spendable currency.
PV — Budget
What you can afford to field. Measured in Alpha Strike Point Value. Spent when equipping formations with specific units.
IP — Influence
Political Clout. Gates what you can request. Earned through battlefield performance, objectives, and political actions. Spent on requisitions and special actions.

Supply & Logistics
Supply is tracked per company. A supply line runs from a DropShip or Supply Depot through connected friendly or neutral hexes to the company's hex. Checked at start of Phase 5.
Weapon Classification — Flagged at List-Building
E — All-Energy
All units carry only energy weapons (lasers, PPCs, flamers). No ammo dependency. Immune to damage die reduction at all supply statuses. Flagged at list-building. Opponent may verify.
M — Mixed
At least one unit has ballistic or missile weapons alongside energy. Partial damage die reduction applies. Avoids ×2 repair cost at strained status.
A — Ammo-Dependent
Primarily or entirely ballistic/missile armed. Full damage die reduction applies when out of supply. Artillery is always A — energy exemption does not apply.

Supply Node Types
DropShip (Primary)
Anchors the supply network. Supplies all friendly companies within 3 hexes automatically. High-value target: +4 IP if destroyed. 2 consecutive barrages to destroy entirely.
Supply Depot (Fixed)
Captured hex node. Extends supply network by +2 hexes from its position. Must trace back to a DropShip. Destroyable: +3 IP. Barrage suppresses it for 1 turn.
Mobile Field Base
Attached to a company. Moves with it. Provides 1-turn emergency supply to its own company only. Single use per campaign turn (Periphery: 2 uses). Costs 10 PV. Destroyed outright by barrage.
Artillery Forward Base
Dedicated supply for artillery lances only. Provides ammo resupply. Artillery without this node loses 1 Damage Die per turn. Priority target: +3 IP. Barrage prevents resupply for 1 turn.
Supply Line Routing
Path must pass through friendly-controlled or uncontested hexes only. Enemy-occupied hexes break the line.

Artillery Supply Interdiction Rules
Artillery range has no bearing on supply interdiction. An artillery unit can only threaten a supply hex if it has direct observation — the battery is 1 hex adjacent to the node, OR a friendly spotter is 1 hex adjacent to the node. Without one of these conditions, the supply hex is safe regardless of range.

Movement Rules
Hex map movement for lances. One hex = one BattleTech map sheet. All movement is declared in Phase 1 before simultaneous resolution.
Atomic unit reminder: A lance moves as a single unit. All 4 units occupy the same hex. Movement decisions are made for the lance, not individual 'Mechs.
Base Movement
1 hex per TMM point
A lance's base hex movement on the world map equals its average TMM value.

TMM 1 → 1 hex/turn  ·  TMM 2 → 2 hexes/turn  ·  TMM 3 → 3 hexes/turn  ·  TMM 4 → 4 hexes/turn  ·  TMM 5 → 5 hexes/turn

Movement and attack can both occur in the same turn unless the lance is Sprinting.
Sprint
Cannot attack · +50% movement (round up) · +50% TMM (round up) for that turn
A lance that declares Sprint in Phase 1 gains significant mobility at the cost of all offensive action that turn.

Sprint movement = base TMM × 1.5, rounded up.
Sprint TMM = base TMM × 1.5, rounded up — applies to defensive targeting modifier for that turn only.

Sprint is declared in Phase 1. A Sprinting lance cannot make any attacks during Phase 3. It may still take damage if engaged.

Movement and Range Interaction
TMM 1–2 — 1 range band/turn
Within a hex battle, these lances move 1 range band per turn (Long → Medium → Short). They can move toward or away from the enemy but only 1 band at a time.

Retreat: Must already be at Long range to exit. Cannot retreat from Medium in one turn.
TMM 3–4 — 2 range bands/turn
Within a hex battle, these lances move up to 2 range bands per turn. Can jump Long → Short in one turn, or retreat from Medium → Long in one turn.

Retreat: Can escape from Medium range in one turn by jumping back to Long, then exiting the following turn.
TMM 5+ — 3 range bands/turn
Within a hex battle, these lances effectively choose any range band each turn. Can retreat from Short range all the way off the map in a single turn. Extremely difficult to pin down — opponents must use terrain or flanking to force engagement.
Sprint Tactical Uses
Flanking approach
A lance sprinting to get behind an enemy gives up its attack that turn but arrives in flanking position — then triggers the free shot and +1 die bonus from the following turn onward. The lost attack turn is usually worth it.
Supply line interdiction
A fast lance (TMM 3, sprinting to 5 hexes) can race deep behind enemy lines to cut a supply line in a single turn — potentially placing a spotter adjacent to an enemy supply depot without the enemy being able to intercept in time.
Escape from artillery threat
A lance that has been spotted for artillery can sprint out of the artillery's observation range (move the spotter's adjacent hex) before the barrage fires in Phase 3. Sprint declares in Phase 1 — the race is simultaneous.
Recon sweep
Recon lances with high TMM can sprint to scan 2 hexes worth of objectives in a single turn — covering more ground than normal movement allows. Scanning still uses the Scan Objective action; the sprint provides the range.
Sprint TMM is defensive only and capped at 5. The increased TMM from Sprinting applies to how hard the lance is to hit that turn — it does not grant additional range band movement within an ongoing hex engagement. Sprint movement applies on the world map between hexes. Range band movement within a battle hex uses the base TMM brackets (1–2, 3–4, 5+). The TMM 5 cap means TMM 4 and TMM 5 lances gain the same defensive benefit from Sprinting — but TMM 5 lances still gain the additional hex movement.
Lance Combat Rules
Range-band engagement, TMM movement within a hex, flanking, and damage resolution.
Range Band System
Every hex engagement plays out across up to 3 range bands: Long → Medium → Short. Lances start at Long range. Each turn the range closes unless a lance uses its movement to hold or retreat. Attacks are resolved at the current range band using that column from the Alpha Strike card totals.
Range BandTypical TurnAttack column usedNotes
LongTurn 1L (Long) totalStarting range for all engagements. Some lances peak here (missile boats, snipers).
MediumTurn 2M (Medium) totalMost balanced range. Majority of lances perform well here.
ShortTurn 3S (Short) totalBrawl range. High-damage lances (brawlers, energy boats) dominate.
TMM and Range Band Movement
Within a hex engagement, TMM determines range band mobility
TMM 1–2 — move 1 range band per turn. Can advance or retreat 1 band. To exit (retreat), must be at Long range.

TMM 3–4 — move 2 range bands per turn. Can jump Long → Short in one turn. Can retreat from Medium to Long in one turn, then exit next turn.

TMM 5+ — move 3 range bands per turn. Effectively chooses any range band freely each turn. Can retreat from Short to off-map in a single turn.
Damage Dice Resolution
Every 2 damage = 1 Damage Die (1 pair of D6)
Read the attack total from the appropriate range column (S / M / L). Divide by 2 to get Damage Dice. Round up for odd totals.

Example: Lance total S: 9 → 4.5 → 5 Damage Dice at Short range.
Example: Lance total L: 4 → 2 Damage Dice at Long range.

Each Damage Die is a pair of D6. Both dice in a pair must meet the target number (based on Skill) to deal damage. If one die in a pair fails, the maximum damage from that pair is allocated to the successful die.
Flanking
Whole lance attacks from behind — two permanent benefits
A flanking engagement requires all 4 units of the lance to be positioned behind the target lance. A single unit behind does not qualify. The GM adjudicates based on hex positioning at the start of the turn.

Benefit 1 — Free shot at any chosen range: Before normal range sequencing begins, the flanking lance picks Short, Medium, or Long and fires once at that range. The enemy cannot react to this shot — it resolves before Phase 3 combat. The flanking lance picks whatever range maximizes its damage output.

Benefit 2 — +1 extra Damage Die ongoing: For every subsequent turn of the engagement, the flanking lance rolls one additional Damage Die on every attack. This bonus persists until the flanking lance retreats or the enemy disengages from the hex.

Countering a flank: A high-TMM lance (3–4) at Medium range can jump back to Long in one turn, then exit the following turn — breaking the engagement before the +1 die bonus compounds. A TMM 5+ lance can break from Short range in a single turn.
Flanking — Worked Example
Setup
Kurita Recon Lance (TMM 3, S:9 M:8 L:1) successfully flanks Davion Fire Support (TMM 2, S:11 M:13 L:18).

Kurita picks Short range for the free shot — their peak damage (9 dmg → 5 dice). Davion's Fire Support has low Short damage (11 dmg → 6 dice) but excels at Long (18 dmg → 9 dice).
Resolution
Free shot (Short): Kurita fires 5 dice. Davion cannot respond.

Turn 1 onward: Engagement starts at Long. Kurita rolls Math.ceil(1/2)+1 = 2 dice (L:1 + flank die). Davion rolls 9 dice (L:18).

Kurita's TMM 3 means it can sprint back to Long next turn if needed — but the +1 die bonus only applies while engaged.
